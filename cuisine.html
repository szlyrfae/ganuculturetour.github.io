MAIN MEALS

    A most sought after dish that is made from a mixture of rice and glutinous rice, steamed twice in coconut milk to get the right texture and eaten with a red tuna fish curry accompanied by pickled cucumber and carrot “acar” or relish. This dish is not for the novice cook as it requires patience and a good eye for detail. One would judge how well nasi dagang is made by a simple test of eating it on its own. If it’s good enough to be eaten on its own, then it is well made!

    Not to be confused with the Kelantanese version, the Terengganu nasi dagang uses white rice and the fish must be tuna. Traditionally, the nasi dagang’s packaging is distinct from other rice dishes in that it is wrapped in banana leaf shaped like a cone; and this is how you identify it when making your choice at a food vendor.

    Nasi Kuning Terengganu is a special yellow rice dish from Terengganu cooked with coconut milk and turmeric, but distinguished by its iconic side dish of Gulai Ikan Tongkol, different from the Indonesian or Sabah versions, making it a legendary local dish often enjoyed with pickled pineapple,sambal belacan, or Terengganu red meat, and not using glutinous rice like glutinous rice.

    This unique dish features blue-colored rice, naturally dyed with butterfly pea flowers (bunga telang), and is complemented by various herbs, sambal, fish or chicken, and crackers.The word kerabu refers to a mix of fresh herbs or salad, which is a key feature of this dish. Nasi Kerabu gets its name because the blue rice is served with kerabu or fresh herbs. The blue color of the rice is associated with the butterfly pea flower, which not only provides the color but is also believed to have health benefits. Historically, the flower was thought to symbolize purity and blessings.

DESSERTS

    Kuih tok aji serban refers to a type of cake cooked with glutinous rice underneath with a sweet sekaya head on top.The head of kuih tok aji serban is made from eggs and coconut milk. It can also be a kuih seri kaya / kuih sekaya if cooked without glutinous rice underneath with only sweet sekaya coconut.

    Kuih Tok Aji Serban is almost similar to kuih Seri Muka except for the kepala mixture (top) which is usually brown (brown) instead of green. This cake is suitable for eating in the evening (afternoon tea).

    Kuih lompat tikam has three components, namely pandan flour pudding which is usually green in color, thick coconut milk which is white in color with a consistency like pudding and red sweet glutinous rice. This part is made plain because it is eaten with gula nisan or gula kabung.

    The name of this traditional cake begins with a story where a maidservant of the King was walking to serve this cake to his Majesty. However, she hit a sari pillar and fell to the floor. Subsequently, the King was very surprised by the sound and jumped and stabbed the maidservant with a keris.

SNACKS

    Keropok Lekor is a traditional Malaysian savory snack, originating from Terengganu, made from minced fish (like mackerel) mixed with sago or tapioca flour, rolled into logs or balls, boiled or steamed, and then typically deep-fried before serving, resulting in a chewy inside with a crispy exterior, often enjoyed with a spicy chili sauce. The name combines "keropok" (crackers) and "lekor" (to roll), describing its form, and it evolved from fishermen preserving excess fish.

    Satar ikan is a popular traditional snack from Terengganu, Malaysia, made from a mixture of fish fillets (usually sardines), grated coconut, and spices (pepper, onion, ginger), wrapped in pyramid-shaped banana leaves, skewered like a satay, and grilled over charcoal. It tastes spicy, fragrant, and fatty, often sold at roadside stalls, especially during the month of Ramadan, and is considered a specialty of the east coast.
